# 🚀 How to Make Your Telegram Bot Public

Great news! Your bot is already **publicly accessible** by its username. Anyone in the world can find and use it by searching for its username in Telegram or by using its direct link (`t.me/YourBotUsername`).

However, making it **discoverable** and look professional is the next step. This guide covers the essential steps to prepare your bot for a public audience, all done through `@BotFather`.

---

### Step 1: Set a Profile Picture

A bot without a profile picture looks unfinished. Let's add one.

1.  **Find a suitable image.** A simple wine glass icon or a logo works well. You can find free icons on sites like [Flaticon](https://www.flaticon.com) or [The Noun Project](https://thenounproject.com).
2.  **Open your chat with `@BotFather`** in Telegram.
3.  Send the command `/mybots` and select your bot (`@YourBotUsername`).
4.  Tap **Edit Bot** -> **Edit Botpic**.
5.  BotFather will ask you to send the photo. Attach the image you chose and send it.

### Step 2: Add a Description

When a user first opens your bot, they see a description of what it does. This is crucial for getting them to press "Start".

1.  In your `@BotFather` chat, go back to the **Edit Bot** menu.
2.  Tap **Edit Description**.
3.  Send a short, clear sentence describing what your bot does. For example:
    > `Your personal sommelier. Scan a wine label or type a name to get its optimal drinking window and expert recommendations.`

### Step 3: Add a Placeholder Text for the Chat

This is the grey text that appears in the message box before the user types anything. It can guide them on what to do.

1.  In the **Edit Bot** menu in `@BotFather`, tap **Edit Description Picture** (this is a confusing name, it should be "Edit About Text").
2.  Send a short text. For example:
    > `I can tell you when to drink your wine. Scan a bottle to get started!`

### Step 4: Define a Command List

This adds a small `/` menu in the chat, making it easy for users to see available commands.

1.  In the **Edit Bot** menu, tap **Edit Commands**.
2.  BotFather requires a specific format: `command1 - description`
3.  Send the following message:
    `start - 🚀 Launch the Wine Assistant`

Now, when a user types `/`, a small pop-up will suggest the `/start` command.

---

### Step 5: Share Your Bot!

Your bot is now ready for the public. The best way to share it is with its direct link.

-   **Your Bot's Link:** `https://t.me/YourBotUsername` (replace `YourBotUsername` with your bot's actual username).

You can post this link on social media, in your email signature, or anywhere else. When someone clicks it, it will open a chat with your bot directly in their Telegram app.

### (Optional) Step 6: Submit to Bot Lists

There are several unofficial (and one official) directories where you can list your bot to help people find it. The most important one is Telegram's own `@BotList`.

1.  **Start a chat with `@BotList`**.
2.  Follow its instructions to submit your bot. You will need to provide the bot's username, a short description, and a long description.
3.  If your bot is approved, it will be featured in the official search and can be discovered by users browsing for new bots.

That's it! Your bot is now fully configured for public use.
